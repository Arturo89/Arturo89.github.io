Hidden City

San José is a city that is hidden behind the abandonment, ignorance and insecurity. There are several examples of historic buildings demolished or impaired. The history of San José is ignored by many citizens and the capital is seen only as a city of passage instead of a real destiny.

It is the lack of knowledge about the history and heritage of San José which make the city unkown to it's citizens.

Historic District

The city also hides a Historic District, which is ignored by many citizens. This center is located between 7th Ave and 10th Ave  and among 9th street and 12th street. As this was the city limits in 1850.

Urban growth and declining population of the area has generated an abandonment of the historic center of the city.

This unknown space houses many heritage buildings.

Architectural Heritage

There are two ways of seeing the heritage, formally it is defined as an legacy, making it official by a Government Statement.

On the other hand, an informal approach defines it as a social construction in which citizens can participate.

Both approaches are concentrated largely in the Historic District. These two definitions narrate in different ways the history of San José.

The Design of the Museo a Cielo Abierto of the City of San José, displaying its architectural heritage through out the design of historic/urban routes and interventions within its historic center routes.

Potential

The declaration: Urban Perimeter of the Historic District of San José states that "there is a need and opportunity to use historic, urban and architectural elements of San José as one of the most important instruments for the development of social and economic regeneration programs and the repopulation of the city as well as a way to promote its culture and touristic potential. "

This declaration seeks to promote the design of projects based on the history and heritage by promoting urban trails in the Historic District.

Urban Museography

"Interpret the city under the concept of a museum, configured by its own architectural, environmental, scenic, and artistic values, by applying museological techniques to the urban space." (Layuno María. 2007)

Museo a Cielo Abierto

The concept of a Museum is applied to the Historic District of San José.

The Museo a Cielo Abierto project works throughout the implementation of thematic routes, to exhibit and promote the heritage of the Historic District of the city.

Ghost Heritage

As an example the Route/Exhibition of the Ghost Heritage is created .

The museum exhibits are held within the city, articulating the urban heritage while guiding and informing the citizens along the exhibition.

San José Visible

Throughout the various exhibitions developed by the MaCa a revaluation process of the heritage begins. The history of the city becomes familiar  to the public. The heritage is no longer seen as a burden but is seen as an added value that attracts the same citizenship.

Working together with the Municipality and other local initiatives, the urban culture in the Historic District gets strengthen.

San José becomes a visible city, which is kept alive by displaying its own history and heritage.
